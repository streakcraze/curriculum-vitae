Types of Job Roles Electronics and Computer Engineering

There is a global demand for competent and qualified electronics and computer engineers. The jobs in the field of electronics and computer technology include building mechanical parts, troubleshooting electrical problems or working with computers. Some of the job profiles that an electronics and computer engineer can opt for are mentioned below:

  • Electrical Engineer: Electrical engineers typically design and/or direct electrical power products and services. They also supervise the design and manufacture of various electronic and computer parts and components.
  • Electrical Technician: Electrical technicians are responsible for helping engineers with the development of electronic and computer equipment. They are responsible for repairing electrical tools and instruments, making quality control reports, drawing diagrams, troubleshooting issues related to design and running diagnostics.
  • Electronic Design Engineers: They design electronic components, software, and other electronic products or systems, inspect electronic equipment to ensure compliance with specification or safety codes and prepare documentation and other information for the electronic equipment.
  • Controls Engineer: A controls engineer work rapidly with the evolving technologies and control systems. The job of a controls engineer is to develop and execute regular engineering processes, work with program design and testing, document the work performed, and troubleshooting.
  • Network Engineers: They work with a company's computer network, using information technology to make network systems for all employees easy to use. They have to create complex blueprints for communication networks and must make sure that all the systems work together.
  • Automation Analysts: They are software developers and computer systems analysts who specialize in software and database quality control. They create automated programs that routinely check computer systems and troubleshoot problems.
  • Computer Support Specialist: Computer support specialists assist organizations, companies, and individuals with computer hardware, software and various equipment. They perform day-to-day maintenance tasks, such as installing programs and conducting computer tests.
